## angles
- Chain of custody logs recording handler identity, moment of transfer, and authorization at issuance time map the contractor laptop incident: unbounded blast radius occurred because no credential-to-handler mapping existed, so response had to be total rather than surgical. Before SOC 2 fieldwork, build an identity-to-credential log at issuance; when the next incident happens, "what needs rotating" becomes a query rather than reconstruction.
- Post-hoc reconstruction of credential distribution (Slack shares, CI variables, vault entries) maps to inadmissible forensic evidence: event-time logging is absent, so access controls cannot be verified by auditors. Reconstructed logs created after the fact cannot be distinguished from confabulation; logs written at issuance time are evidentiary and defensible.
- Hardcoded strings in repo history and other untracked credentials map to uninventoried evidence: they exist but have no chain because they were never logged into the system, making their provenance permanently opaque. Rotation cadence, vault, and CI hygiene only work on inventoried credentials; the first move is an evidence audit to discover what exists before establishing custody process.
