## angles
- Documentary editors pull peak moments from footage and place them at the front as a hook before any setup -> your constraint (hours of footage, no reshoots, rapid audience attention) mirrors this exactly -> watch the full rough cut, tag the single ten-second clip that made you feel something, drop it at frame zero with no introduction
- Researchers isolate which variable is causing a result through controlled variation -> your impression drop is ambiguous because thumbnails, titles, and topics are all mixed across recent videos -> treat your channel as a running experiment with a three-cell design: test two thumbnail aesthetics on videos one and two while keeping title style constant, then test two title structures on videos two and three while keeping thumbnail style constant, gathering overlapping controls
- Line cooks stage everything before service begins (mise en place) to work within a finite prep window with no ability to undo -> your editing constraint mirrors this: no reshoots once filming ends -> before touching the timeline, spend twenty minutes writing three sentences about what the piece is, what moment carries the most visual surprise, and what the viewer will understand after watching, then use that as your edit decision filter to cut anything that doesn't serve those sentences
- Industrial designers use affordance signals — visual cues that tell you how to use something -> thumbnails and titles are affordance signals for what kind of experience is inside, read in ~300 milliseconds -> your impression drop may reflect a shift in genre legibility rather than quality; pulling your five lowest and five highest impression thumbnails to compare genre-signal differences rather than quality differences might reveal whether your aesthetic has shifted from "satisfying process video" toward "talking-head opinion video"
