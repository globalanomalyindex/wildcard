## angles
- Quit-and-restart happens at the moment engagement drops (familiar territory), and the exit path points back to starting over. This is a structural exit point, not a motivation problem. If you enter the orbit past the familiar zone, the tangent exit would point through the hard material instead of around it.
- The felt resistance at the boundary between known and unknown (bass clef, syncopation) is the felt experience of being at the edge of competence, not evidence of failure. The felt pull toward familiar pieces is the sharpest corner of the orbit, not a signal to turn back.
- A complete learning orbit has a specific shape: start from your current edge, move into unfamiliar territory, push until something shifts, return with the new thing partially absorbed. The question is not the most ambitious arc you can start, but the tightest one you can finish and return from in your available time (20 focused minutes).
