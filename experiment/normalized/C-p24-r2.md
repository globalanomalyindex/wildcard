## angles
- cold opens tested in isolation with fresh audiences before knowing the full context -> body language and dropout points reveal whether the problem is structure, pacing, or promise -> run five cold opens unlisted with no context and share with a small group to diagnose the retention cliff
- evaluation of thumbnails, titles, and topics happens simultaneously, preventing diagnosis of cause -> triage by cheapest-to-test-first, since titles cost nothing to change retroactively while topics cost a full week -> run A/B title tests on underperforming videos before assuming the topic is wrong
- editing burden of 20:1 footage ratio creates an upstream decision problem -> documentary editors use a "selects pass" rule where they flag only the 10 percent worth keeping before opening the NLE -> refuse to touch the timeline until all footage is logged once, cutting editing hours in half
- viewers leave in the first 45 seconds because no micro-story establishes what's at stake -> Joseph Campbell's hero journey structure works at any scale -> state the problem the piece solves and show one moment of failure or uncertainty from later in the build, giving viewers a reason to stay past second 45
