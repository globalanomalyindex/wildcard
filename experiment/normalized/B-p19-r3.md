## angles
- Recycling facilities surface contamination signals at collection time rather than after failed batches; decoupled volume and recoverable yield are solved by making the hidden measurement legible early -> Trial users' host count and experienced load are similarly decoupled; they feel things are working without knowing host allocation is nearly full until the wall hits -> Surface host-utilization readout from day one, not as a warning at 100% but as a running metric, to see if earlier legibility changes how users self-select tiers.
- Wish-cycling occurs because the signal enabling correct sorting (is this recyclable?) isn't present at the decision point -> Trial users wish-tier by picking cheapest plan based on hope rather than measurement, because host-count signal isn't present at signup -> Move the diagnostic to the decision point: ask "how many servers or containers are you monitoring?" at trial signup and surface which tier fits, so the moment of measurement aligns with the moment of choice.
- Recycling programs reported tonnage collected (easy to count) while yield (actually recovered and sold) quietly diverged; the proxy metric was optimized while economically meaningful outcomes failed -> Finance models per-host revenue while trial-to-paid conversion is what's actually failing; the system optimizes for the proxy -> Surface both metrics side-by-side for finance: host revenue per cohort alongside trial conversion rate, so the cost of unit misalignment with user mental models becomes visible in economic terms.
