## angles
- Pricing is structured as inverse encodings of infrastructure reality (hosts per tier vs. requests per month), but customers read only positive form and collapse on price heuristics → users need a translation layer on the pricing page that maps each tier's host limit to its approximate requests-per-month equivalent derived from actual customer usage data → add a legend that wraps the pricing impression with words the recipient can read, without changing the billing unit itself
- The trial signup is an impression moment where the substrate must be prepared before the choice sets, or fine structure collapses → users arrive without a mental model for host-based pricing and buy on cheapness instead → ask one orienting question early in trial signup ("roughly how many API calls per month?") to warm the substrate by translating the user's volume into host count before tier choice is made
- Full calculator exposure of unit economics is a constraint, but trial users churn from exceeded limits they didn't anticipate, not from unfavorable small-scale rates → partial legend covering only mid-to-large bands (where unit is favorable) solves the majority problem without publishing small-scale comparisons competitors can exploit → selective translation serves authentication function for the intended customer band without exposing full grammar to other parties
