## angles
- Baseline tape that develops cumulative creep while appearing precise -> memorized pieces learned without notation reading are laid down without the calibration instrument needed, so they cannot propagate -> identify the specific properties that are wrong (bass clef and syncopation) and make a minimum correction rather than adding more pieces to a compromised chain
- Re-measuring segments that were already correct in a triangulation chain -> restarting beginner books re-works the part of the chain you already measured correctly, delaying arrival at where the chain actually broke -> begin at the exact point where the error first exceeded tolerance (bass clef fluency and rhythmic patterns) rather than at the beginning
- Uncertainty budget as working knowledge -> you have already characterized where your chain is trustworthy and where it isn't (six pieces solid; bass clef slow and error-prone; syncopation fragile; rhythm in novel contexts unreliable) -> use this documented budget to direct what to practice in a given session, knowing exactly which segment needs measurement
