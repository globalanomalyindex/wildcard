## angles
- False cognates across "host" (your unit) and "request" (customer's mental model) → absence of translation layer means mismatch persists from landing through signup → build a one-page, no-branding translation tool that converts "X apps making Y requests/day" to "roughly N hosts," teachable via onboarding email
- Trial users churn when hitting host limits without understanding the constraint → like ski rental shops that ask fit questions before equipment checkout, a two-question onboarding gate ("how many services?" and "shared vs. dedicated?") surfaces tier recommendations before signup and protects users from under-buying
- Exit surveys reporting "felt tricked" as signal without product constraint → treat the failure mode itself as design input: a live host-count indicator during trial (visible gas gauge, not alarm at empty) lets users self-upgrade on day two rather than churn
- "Hosts" as implementation detail rather than primary anchor → lead landing page, calculator, and trial copy entirely in requests-per-month, introduce hosts only as technical detail in FAQ, test whether the delayed reframing feels like bait-and-switch (risk mitigated by two-sentence explanation of the host-to-request translation)
