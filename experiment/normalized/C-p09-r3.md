## angles
- Air traffic flow management uses departure slots to spread peak demand across a window rather than blocking flights. Stop thinking about rate limits and start thinking about reservation slots — let enterprise tenants' scheduled export jobs request a 10-minute window from a lightweight scheduler that spreads them across the hour, giving you load leveling and a paper trail for SLA conversations.
- Power grid demand pricing surfaces price signals to let customers self-optimize; here, surface a latency forecast to enterprise tenants through an API or dashboard (expected p99 by time) so their Airflow/dbt schedulers voluntarily shift peak exports, and generate internal leverage for engineering to point out the signal already exists.
- Emergency medicine triage tags every patient at intake before they reach resources, making everything downstream possible. Reliably attach tenant ID and query-weight estimate at the HTTP gateway or ClickHouse's query settings before touching throttling logic; 30 days of attributed load data will give you the business case for isolated clusters.
- Ecological succession: pioneer species exploit open resources until shade-tolerant species build a stable canopy. Add a lightweight query queue in front of ClickHouse (like ClickHouse's workload management or chproxy) that imposes per-tenant concurrency cap without perfect tenant ID threading; even a coarse cap on simultaneous queries per IP blunts the spike while you build proper attribution in parallel.
