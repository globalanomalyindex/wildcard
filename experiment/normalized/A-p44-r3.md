## angles
- Beginner book creates a closed drainage basin: locally familiar material flows back into itself, blocking access to desired destinations (bass clef fluency, syncopation) that sit outside the basin topology -> diagnosis is not "try harder" but recognizing that restarting the same path cannot reach a different watershed -> route directly to isolated destinations separately, treating them as immediate five-minute slots before familiar practice, not destinations arrived at eventually
- Syncopation is a threshold (binary capacity for independent pulse streams) rather than a hill (gradable difficulty) -> threshold-crossing requires system separation and abrupt stabilization, not incremental easing -> establish left hand pulse in isolation, right hand pulse in isolation, then merge; the crossing is uncomfortable and then complete
- Bass clef reading deficit is ponded in a zone with no natural outlet within the closed basin -> practical intervention is channelization: cut a narrow, steep path that enters first and non-negotiably before familiar material can absorb the session -> five minutes of bass-clef flashcards or single-hand reading exercise at the start of every session, not at the end when time is gone, routes water into a different drainage direction
