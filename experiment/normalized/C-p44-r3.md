## angles
- Six memorized pieces as cached motor programs drop non-matching input (bass clef, offbeat rhythms) like video codec compression artifacts -> the issue isn't skill ceiling but representation mismatch -> transcribe a single measure from existing repertoire into staff notation to rebuild the bridge between motor knowledge and symbolic knowledge
- Silent time after 8pm is a constraint analogous to missing audio in closed captions -> the intervention is channel-shifting rather than content reduction -> use silent practice (air piano, MIDI keyboard, body percussion) to train the timing system in a non-acoustic channel
- Restarting beginner books and bailing out is a navigation failure, not motivation failure -> spaced-repetition systems address this by surfacing only material at the edge of competence -> build an Anki deck with one card per difficult measure, bass-clef exercise, and syncopation pattern to eliminate beginner-book page-skipping
- Jazz pedagogy inverts the beat-landing frame: the beat is a wall and syncopation is the space between -> Clark Terry's "imitate, assimilate, innovate" starts with listening -> practice singing the rhythm of offbeats against a metronome click to reframe syncopation from "almost missing the beat" to "intentionally landing elsewhere"
