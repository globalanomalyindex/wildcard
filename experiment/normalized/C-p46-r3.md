## angles
- Field biologists log encounters rather than pre-catalog → personal meal log captures what you actually made better than saved links → keep a running weekly log (dish name, source, who liked it, substitutions) searchable in one place
- Pharmacies track depletion not inventory eyeballing → use a standing "gone or almost gone" household list added to as things are consumed → avoids duplicate-spice problem without requiring full pantry audit
- Jazz musicians separate standards (15-20 reliable dishes) from deep cuts → organize meal collection into two tiers rather than undifferentiated saves → standards are defaults, Instagram saves needn't be organized at all
- Archivists describe containers not items → create one-page finding aid for grandmother's binder by category (soups, pastas, sides) instead of digitizing each card → index travels with you, binder stays at parents' house
