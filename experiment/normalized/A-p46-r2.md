## angles
- Items enter from five distinct sources (bookmarks, Instagram, screenshots, notes, handwritten cards) but provenance is stripped at capture, leaving an undifferentiated mass with no source signature, date tried, or context tags → source-environment tagging at capture is non-negotiable for later correlation → establish a lightweight capture habit with minimum source signature fields (three or four fields that let you find and filter what you captured).
- The collection exists but has no time-ordered core structure, making recency queries fail even though the material is there → without a continuous index preserving time-order, you can't answer "what was the thing I made three weeks ago that everyone liked" → build a single canonical location where anything tested gets a one-line entry (date, name, verdict, where the full recipe lives) to serve as the core that makes your silos queryable.
- Dead links and paywalled bookmarks remain in the collection looking valid until the moment you actually need them, when they return nothing under pressure → the artifact is indistinguishable from the real thing until retrieval failure → do verification work at capture or shortly after, not at retrieval; a saved link with a quick check and local copy of key information becomes primary material instead of a reworked grain, changing the failure rate on everything going forward.
