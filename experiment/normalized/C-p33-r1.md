## angles
- Ant colonies coordinate foraging through pheromone trails that reinforce good routes and fade on bad ones → stigmergy provides indirect coordination without a central dispatcher → lightweight end-of-day logs marking zip codes and parts used create a heat map over weeks that enables pre-positioning technicians toward clustering demand zones at day-start
- Hospital pharmacies manage inventory with par levels per location rather than real-time tracking → vans need par levels calibrated to their usual territory and call type, not uniform across fleet → derive initial par lists from technicians' recall of their last thirty calls, then adjust as patterns emerge
- Medical blood-bank networks address summer-surge geometry with satellite depots at geographic midpoints stocked with fast-movers → small unmanned cache positioned near densest summer call cluster cuts repositioning time for serving technicians → parts need proximity to demand, not residence on truck
- Theater production managers track props at two levels: committed to active show versus available in warehouse → a visible-committed layer (technician writes name and job on card, clips to part) creates real-time visibility without software → untagged parts become the trustworthy inventory count and surface the failure mode of parts on trucks committed to no current job
