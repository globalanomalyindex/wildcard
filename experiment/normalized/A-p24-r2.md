## angles
- Treating without triaging first (editing on contact without a rapid coarse pre-pass) wastes capacity on first-encountered problems while high-yield footage remains untagged -> a dedicated fast sort pass (tagging footage into use/maybe/don't-touch bins without actual editing) before any treatment reduces the effective scope to only the "use it" pile -> implement a 20-minute sort pass before the editing session
- The 45-second viewer bleed is an assembly problem, not a footage problem (opening clips exist in the 4-6 hours but haven't been sorted forward) -> coarse diagnosis under throughput pressure means treating the accessible wound first rather than waiting for perfect diagnosis -> run a dedicated "find the hook" pass (5 minutes, existing footage only, looking only for clips with immediate motion or tension) to solve the opening problem now
- Three candidate causes for stall (thumbnail, title, topic) with different reversibilities exist, but the diagnostic instinct blocks action -> sort by reversibility, not by diagnosis, and treat in that order (thumbnails and titles changeable post-upload; topic is fixed at film time) -> test thumbnails first (most accessible, swappable today, generates one-week closure on that variable rather than months of pattern-waiting)
