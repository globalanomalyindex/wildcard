## angles
- credential inventory problem from SAR last-known-position doctrine → establish bounded blast radius instead of total recall → build credential registry mapping secrets to location, scope, and last verification (answering SOC 2 inventory requirement without dev friction)
- access control from epidemiology index-case tracing → conflating exposure discovery with blanket rotation → model access as minimal contact lists per identity, rotate only what the compromised identity could touch
- credential scoping from commercial real estate master-key tenant-key distinction → everything at master-key level increases blast radius on any compromise → categorize secrets as tenant-keys (scoped, short-lived) vs master-keys (broad, long-lived), vault the master-keys first, defer tenant-keys to .env to reduce dev friction
- SOC 2 compliance from archival significant-properties framework → treating all secrets equally is inefficient triage → identify five to ten genuinely catastrophic credentials, vault those now, accept "good enough" on everything else (auditors verify you know what matters and have controls on it)
