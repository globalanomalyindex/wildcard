## angles
- Non-linear calibration curves create "plateaus" where different inputs map to nearly identical outputs; the ambiguity lives in curve shape, not measurement noise -> in pricing, hosts and requests lack linear relationship and diverge sharply at small scale, creating poor discriminating power exactly where trial users operate -> make the non-linearity visible at trial start by reporting where current usage sits on the host-to-request curve and what the next step looks like, rather than collapsing to false certainty
- Time series patterns can carry information that single-point measurements cannot -> trial users generate request growth trajectories (velocity, shape) that map to predictable positions on host-limit curves and become visible by day three -> surface a behavioral trigger on trajectory shape proactively rather than waiting for threshold collision, using the shape of early growth to place users on the curve before ambiguity resolves into a problem
- Model calibration uncertainty often exceeds and masks measurement uncertainty, leading to reported precision overstating actual accuracy -> in the finance model, host count as a revenue proxy carries calibration assumption uncertainty that is likely large at small scale where the curve is steep -> present the model's own confidence interval at small-customer segments by showing where calibration error exceeds measurement error, reframing within finance's existing error-bounds vocabulary
