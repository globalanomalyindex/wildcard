## angles
- The three suspected causes (thumbnail, title, topic) have been varying simultaneously across videos, making the observation confounded -> A physicist designs the next experiment to hold two variables constant and vary only one at a time -> Run deliberate test windows: three or four videos with identical topic and title but varied thumbnail, then a run varying only title, to get diagnostic readings that actually separate the signal from ambiguous observations.
- Video assembly involves 4-6 hours of footage dominated by "background": walk-to-bench, tool setup, glue cure waiting, second takes -> Most fields can't eliminate background noise (detectors use cosmic ray muons, radioactive decay, neutrino interactions), so instead they model what background looks like -> Build a crisp taxonomy of what "background footage" looks like in your workflow (classes of shots always cut, durations always too long), then recognition becomes the task rather than evaluation.
- The retention graph shows not just "30% left in first 45 seconds" but which specific second or five-second bin where the drop is steepest -> The geometry of a signal encodes its cause; a cliff at 3-8 seconds points at expectation mismatch (thumbnail/title promise vs. opening), while a cliff at 15-25 seconds points at pacing -> Read where inside the window the slope is steepest, not just the endpoint, to diagnose which part of the 45 seconds is doing the work.
