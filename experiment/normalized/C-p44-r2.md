## angles
- Isolated skill deficits in bass clef and syncopation treated as separate daily drills (flashcard apps, silent practice) -> decouples reading from performance so they can level up on different schedules -> practice specific mechanics blocking progress rather than restarting from the beginning
- Passive exposure to bass clef notation (tracing printed scores, naming notes aloud) before active keyboard production -> builds mental mapping of note patterns without performance pressure -> silent pre-practice fits scheduling constraints and accelerates pattern recognition
- Syncopation as a call-and-response conversation between the off-beat and the on-beat silence, with the rest as the active moment -> reframes internal timing and reduces cognitive load of subdividing -> clap the figure as a two-person exchange before touching keys
- The exact three problematic measures in a loved piece as the entire curriculum, isolated via slow-down tools and apps -> treats the specific gap as the scope rather than following sequential beginner-book logic -> focuses motivation and pacing on the actual problem
