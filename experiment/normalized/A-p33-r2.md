## angles
- Local validity hides multi-attribute constraints (nearest available tech masks skill and inventory mismatch) -> this collapses matching across multi-dimensional state just like dispatching a pilot with insufficient duty time or missing type rating looks valid in isolation but breaks downstream -> prefilter candidate sets by job type and tech capability before proximity sort, building a legal-pairings matrix like airline irregular-ops does, if job-type differentiation is high enough to make the matrix tractable
- Every assignment repositions capacity for tomorrow (vans end the day scattered across the metro, starting positions determine next-morning drive time debt) -> greedy dispatch that ignores terminal position compounds into geometric repositioning cost, just as crew-member end-of-day location determines next-day deadheading -> use geographically closer-to-home as a tiebreaker for the last one or two jobs of each tech's day to close the loop toward depot and avoid cascading debt
- Callbacks are capacity leaks, not new demand (a second visit is a failed first job competing during the surge period when capacity is most constrained, not a fresh job) -> treating them as normal demand items lets them drift to the back of the queue and amplifies damage, structurally identical to an incomplete flight leg re-queued during recovery -> flag callbacks as priority-above-new-demand in a separate elevated queue, assigned as the first job of the closest equipped van, giving measurement of which job types and part gaps generate the drain
- Constraint-checking work moves from crisis improvisation to low-stakes precomputation (parts-matching validation the night before, given scheduled calls and van inventory, is cheap; improvised callbacks during surge are expensive) -> stress-testing swap patterns at low-stakes time rather than solving under pressure reduces both execution errors and downstream surprises -> run a pre-kitting pass before the day begins to flag mismatched jobs and either rebalance assignments or trigger parts pulls, converting an unreliable demand signal into a forward-looking pull list
