## angles
- The system reads transmitter state (return event) rather than field condition (physical reality in the yard) -> the monitor shows nominal while the course is bent, because the inspection gap between return and completed check is where corruption lives -> only mark an asset available upon completed physical inspection, never upon return alone; this makes the gap visible rather than masked
- High-traffic periods don't just overload the system; they degrade the signal quality of each individual measurement -> inspections rushed under pressure are noisier and propagate more errors, like transient deformations during peak traffic -> identify the minimum valid check sequence that remains signal-clean even under pressure, rather than just accelerating the full sequence
- The utilization metric likely tracks aggregate health while the real problem—the ratio within each asset class—bends underneath -> like an ILS that tracks signal strength instead of the ratio between two lobes, the aggregate turns-per-period stays healthy while cleared:total by asset class falls out of tolerance -> track cleared:available separately by asset class and watch that ratio rather than the aggregate to expose where lobes are out of tolerance
- The only way to certify a signal is to physically traverse the space it occupies; no monitor sensitivity or proxy replaces physical presence -> for the yard, the verifying agent must traverse the subject and only a completed physical inspection should flip an asset from returned to available
