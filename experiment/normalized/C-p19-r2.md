## angles
- FDA nutrition labeling added "per container" columns alongside "per serving" to surface the unit users think in, without changing the underlying measurement unit or regulatory model -> your constraint is that finance models per-host and the unit can't change, but you can surface the unit that users think in alongside the one you sell in -> add a "your estimated monthly requests" field on the pricing page feeding into live tier recommendation; map common ranges to tier recommendations and show the math openly ("~3 hosts handles 5M req/mo")
- Apartment listings show total move-in cost alongside monthly rent, which reduced "felt tricked" sentiment by eliminating surprise at the decision point -> your churn is driven by felt surprise of hitting a host limit, a disclosure problem happening at the trial wall -> build an in-product "host gauge" from day one of trial showing current hosts used vs. tier limit with projected date-to-limit based on usage trajectory
- AWS, GCP, and Stripe reduced churn with spend estimators and alert thresholds; users who set budget alerts churned at half the rate of those who didn't, even at the same cost ceiling -> trial users experience capacity shock when an invisible limit becomes visible after they've built something; the solution is making the limit visible before it's hit -> during onboarding ask users to estimate deployment size and show projection: "At that scale, you'd be on our [Tier X] plan. Your trial gives you [Y]% of that capacity"
- Airlines made the exchange rate between dollars and miles legible at purchase by showing miles earned on checkout, so users think in two units simultaneously without friction -> you have two currencies: hosts (how you sell) and requests (how users think); you don't need to unify them, you need to make the exchange rate legible in context -> on every tier card show the requests-equivalent in plain language as secondary copy: "Up to 3 hosts — handles roughly 8M requests/month for typical workloads"
