## angles
- Toyota's milk-run parts replenishment (fixed circuit, scheduled pickup/delivery vs. demand-triggered crisscross) -> zones reduce dispatch chaos and consolidate travel -> try two-tech test zone with fixed van territories and midday parts runner circuit
- Hospital pharmacy formulary (tiered parts availability: high-frequency items everywhere, rare items sourced on demand) -> 18 months of callback data likely concentrates in ~30 SKUs that resolve 80% of second visits -> define van formulary from frequency analysis and stage complex-job parts separately
- Emergency triage pre-assessment (severity classified before arrival, not after, to allocate resources early) -> structured three-question phone screen before scheduling can pre-sort calls into van-stock, staged-parts, or specialist-installer categories -> trial pre-screening on new calls to see if callback rates shift
- CDN edge-cache placement (resource proximity more valuable than central consolidation) -> best installer's drive time is a centralization problem; satellite staging in service-area clusters reduces consolidation overhead -> test whether geographic job clustering vs. shop location justifies a satellite bay or locked trailer
