## angles
- Speedrunners isolate and drill the hardest section first rather than following linear progression -> beginner books force linear paths through familiar territory -> extract the two specific problems (bass clef reading, syncopation) as standalone skill sprints, practicing bass clef identification 15 minutes nightly for two weeks via flashcards rather than graded books
- Martial arts students learn katas forwards and backwards, not just forwards -> you already own six memorized pieces motorically but cannot read bass clef -> use remembered pieces as bass clef reading material by printing scores and tracing only the left-hand staff while the right hand plays from memory
- Industrial designers treat tight constraints as gifts that force creative specificity -> the silent-after-8pm window is a constraint -> use this time for mental practice: vividly imagine playing passages, hear them internally, trace fingerings with pencil on paper, which sports science shows produces retention gains comparable to physical practice
- Language acquisition happens through comprehensible input slightly above current level, not from grammar drills -> beginner piano books are far below your level and syncopation appears only in formal exercises -> find YouTube transcriptions of a song you love with natural syncopation that you can almost play, learn it slowly and badly for a month to encounter the rhythmic pattern hundreds of times in a context you care about
