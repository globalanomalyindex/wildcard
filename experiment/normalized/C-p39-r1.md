## angles
- Air traffic controllers pre-assign departure windows to prevent surge queues. The Saturday morning crush is unmanaged demand hitting fixed capacity. Pre-assign return slot times at the point of rental so the inspection crew's throughput becomes predictable and customers missing their slot join the back.
- Hospital emergency departments use color-coded triage tags for instant status visibility. System "available" status diverges from physical yard availability. Physical hang-tags on machines (red for awaiting inspection, yellow for fueling/cleaning, green for ready) make status visible without system lookup and create accountability.
- Restaurant line cooks complete prep work before service rush. Inspection work is treated as post-return, but much could front-load. A pre-return checklist texted to customers the day before lets mechanics triage on arrival: customer-verified clean units go to express inspection, disputed units go to full inspection.
- Retail buyers analyze inventory by turnover age, not just sell-through percentage. Unsold six-month-old stock is a different problem than weekly restocked inventory. A "days since last return" column sorted descending surfaces dead excavators and prompts conversations about pricing, market fit, or contractual holds that aggregate utilization numbers mask.
