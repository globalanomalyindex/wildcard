## angles
- The lab notebook model (from scientific field research): can't triangulate back to a recipe when it's needed -> keep a running index with date, dish name, one flavor word, and location pointer -> write one line each time you cook something you like
- The costume designer's continuity sheet (from film production): screenshots and saves are trying to be both the document and the proof, failing at both -> treat the screenshot as traceability proof, not the recipe itself, with annotation or month-based folder -> can always find the full recipe later once you know it exists
- The mechanic's parts manifest (from auto repair): spice-rebuying is a parts manifest problem, not a recipe problem -> separate from meal planning entirely and use a single photo of the spice shelf taken Sunday morning -> manifest doesn't need to be complete to cut most waste
- The oral tradition compression technique (from folk culture): the dish you made three weeks ago has one or two sensory signatures you remember but can't search for -> record in compressed language first like "the crispy chickpea thing with lemon and harissa" -> reconstruct from a strong description instead of needing to archive the full recipe
