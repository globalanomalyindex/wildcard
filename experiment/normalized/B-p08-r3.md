## angles
- Every alert that doesn't drive actionable response still depletes engineer capacity; the 90% non-actionable pages are load that competes with the 10% critical pages -> frame noise-reduction as capacity-preservation, not just efficiency
- The system has already shown cracking (resignation citing on-call load) and pre-crack indicators (phone muting); failure modes appear late, after the metal is already unsafe -> track accumulated non-actionable pages per engineer per period as a leading metric for burnout, not waiting for visible departure
- Cannot raise alert thresholds globally without knowing which ones guard regulated payment paths; instead of resolving that everywhere, identify alerts that sit on critical compliance paths (likely a short list), annotate those explicitly, and treat everything unclassified as safe for threshold loosening -> start with a targeted audit: which specific alerts are load-bearing for payment-failure paths?
