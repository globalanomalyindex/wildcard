## angles
- Irreversibility of edits (glued-up piece cannot be reshot) mirrors irreversibility in air traffic control sequencing; decision-making must move upstream to capture phase rather than post-production phase, treating the camera as a sequencing tool and capturing hook shots deliberately before glue-up rather than hoping they ended up in footage -> capture decisions should be made by the person who will edit, not whoever held the camera -> decide what the edit needs to show in the first 45 seconds and shoot it explicitly while it still exists, as a deliberate act
- Edit load grows because there's no structural constraint on footage intake volume, no designed separation minimum between capture and post -> the pipeline lacks a hard shoot plan with maximum footage duration and pre-written sequence of named shots that map to edit structure -> establish a separation minimum analogue: shoot against a hard plan of maximum 90 minutes total footage with five to seven named shots per video, reducing edit time structurally by designing intake rate before the shoot
- Viewer retention shows a gate at 45 seconds where 30% depart, structurally analogous to the stabilization gate in approach (gear down, power set by 1,000 feet) where the commitment to continue must already be decided -> the signal that stabilizes the viewer's decision must arrive before the gate closes -> the build needs to be visible (not described, visible) before 45 seconds in the form of the finished piece, the specific problem the jig solves, or the first physical action of the build
