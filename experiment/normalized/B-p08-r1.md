## angles
- All thirty alerts arrive in the same channel at the same apparent urgency with no gradient expressed -> a gradient must be made visible before directed flow is possible -> restore the criticality gradient (e.g., two-tier separation so regulated-path failures land in one channel and threshold-check noise lands in another); check whether the gradient exists in alert metadata and surface it to people on call
- The aggregate statistic of "30 alerts per week, 10% actionable" is masking a bimodal population underneath -> decompose to see which alert types are reliably actionable and which are nearly always noise -> read per-alert-type actionability rates from postmortem records to act surgically rather than tuning the whole population at once
- Batch-job CPU and disk thresholds were calibrated without a model of normal batch-job operation, sitting near or inside the noise floor of regular activity -> characterize the distribution of CPU and disk specifically during known batch windows as the noise floor, then teach the alert what background looks like -> apply time-gated thresholds that are elevated during batch windows and return to sensitive defaults outside them, leaving regulated-path sensitivity untouched
