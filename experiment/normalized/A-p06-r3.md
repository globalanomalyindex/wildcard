## angles

- When custody documentation has a gap, contamination propagates forward from the break point. The "rotate everything" response is structurally correct for a broken chain — the blast radius is unbounded without a break-point timestamp. -> Building SOC 2 audit trails isn't about better incident response; it's about establishing documented floors on blast radius so next time you know exactly what needs rotation, not everything.

- Chain of custody must be established at the moment of transfer (contemporaneous documentation), not reconstructed after the fact. Post-hoc testimony about what probably happened is not equivalent. -> When engineers push back on provisioning overhead, the forensic argument is that there is no other window the record can be written in; retroactive reconstruction is structurally impossible once the moment passes.

- Every store holding a credential is a custodian and a link in the chain that must be documented. Audit is expensive and fragile; eliminating custodians is structurally cleaner. -> Consolidate where credentials live rather than auditing every undocumented custodian (.env files, CI variables, shared vaults, hardcoded strings) — fewer hands on an exhibit means shorter chains to defend.
