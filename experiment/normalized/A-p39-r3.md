## angles
- Intake records are incomplete (photos, timestamps, damage notes are inconsistent or missing) -> Without standardized records at point of entry, disputes about asset condition become unresolvable and downstream decisions inherit the error -> Implement a mandatory, identical intake checklist that must be completed before a return leaves the gate, making the intake record a binding contract between assessor and downstream providers
- Returns are processed first-come-first-served during surge periods -> Under constraint (Saturday 7–9am surge), this rule maximizes time spent on complex cases while quick-turnaround units queue behind them, wasting throughput -> Separate "cosmetically dirty, mechanically sound" from "needs mechanic time" at intake and route quick-turnaround units to the front of the inspection queue during surge, recovering more rental-ready assets in the window
- Intake and inspection are undifferentiated (both mechanics receive any return in arrival order and perform full inspection regardless of actual need) -> The most demanding cases enter the treatment pipeline first and starve flow for routine units -> Introduce a lightweight intake sort step with a fixed checklist that routes assets into lanes (quick-release vs. mechanic-hold), keeping mechanics focused only on what requires them
- Assets marked "available" with no further status remain invisible to review systems -> Untagged or incorrectly categorized assets accumulate and age silently without triggering action, their deterioration hidden by acceptable aggregate metrics -> Introduce a named deferral status (e.g., deferred — review at day 14) with a systematic review trigger, making invisible aging visible to the system
