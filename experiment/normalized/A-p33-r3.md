## angles
- Resolution comes from the arrangement (baselines) not individual nodes; fleet first-contact completion is currently limited by inventory isolation -> treating coverage as a network problem where deep inventory at designated trucks maximizes part availability to any job location
- True inventory state is reconstructed from sparse, biased sampling (only logging failures) which produces phantom beliefs about stock distribution; sidelobes in this mental model persist without validation -> denser, better-distributed sampling by logging every job outcome (parts used, substituted, absent) to reconstruct the true distribution of parts demand and presence
- Summer surge decorrelates the system from its implicit reference model (four calls/day, current parts mix, current territories); dispatch runs on stale assumptions while techs drift into new zones and parts consumption shifts -> short, frequent re-calibration during surge season (e.g., weekly) to re-examine actual service patterns and re-stock, adjusting territories before drift accumulates
