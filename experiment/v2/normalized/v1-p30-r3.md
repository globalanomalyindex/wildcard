## angles
- Floor layout reflects receiving history, not velocity optimization -> warehouse slots are assigned by arrival order like reef organisms settling in sequence -> move top 10–15% of SKUs by pick frequency into dense zones near pack stations (velocity-tiered slotting) over two or three weeks
- Receiving clusters on Monday and Thursday are disturbance events; recovery window may not close before next pulse -> if pick aisles don't fully clear by Wednesday, the constraint is the interval between inbound pulses, not just volume per event -> smooth receiving appointments toward Tuesday and Wednesday to stretch the interval and lower the peak
- Floor workflows (pick traffic, inbound pallets, staging, transit) compete for the same undifferentiated aisle space -> structural heterogeneity (defined buffer zones, insulated hot-pick zones, transit lanes kept clear) decouples competing workflows without requiring more square footage -> paint lines and commit to functional separation between distinct workflow channels
