## angles
- Observed water level looks like 140 irregularities, but underneath are maybe 12 constituent forces that generate the whole surface; the 140 WCAG failures are a surface signal with underlying root-cause categories (missing labels, insufficient color-contrast tokens, broken focus order, missing ARIA roles, session/timeout handling) → before any remediation sprint, build a spreadsheet mapping all 140 failures to root-cause category and whether each requires legal re-approval or is code-only, so you can sequence the nine months against actual bottleneck structure rather than failure count
- Two constituent frequencies that are close enough alias their energy bleeds together into one apparent signal; failures filed under different WCAG success criteria may be the same root cause and grouping them separately means you fix them separately at three times the cost → audit the failure list for clusters where SC 2.2.1, SC 3.3.1, and SC 1.3.1 failures appear on the same screens, especially form-heavy slow-loading screens; a session-extension warning triggered at 12-minute mark plus server-side preservation of form-field state on session expiry addresses all three without touching form wording
- The M2 constituent often accounts for 70-80% of total signal amplitude in mid-latitude harbor tidal prediction; the shared header code that nobody owns is almost certainly your highest-amplitude constituent with a missing skip link or broken focus trap creating a failure on all 60-plus screens → build the constituent table from move 1, count how many of the 140 failures trace to the header, and bring the count to the vendor coordination meeting framed as a request to assign header stewardship with a 30-day remediation window, showing that resolving ownership changes the scoreboard faster than screen-by-screen triage
