## angles
- Shared overhead (canopy) with differentiation below is the architecture at play -> the organization may need one mark at the canopy level and acknowledged variation below rather than treating two marks as competitors -> name them as different things (chapter-level program identity vs. organizational identity), not as broken vs. correct versions
- Umbrella failure concentrates at the perimeter, not the center -> mark fragmentation (nine-plus versions) likely follows this pattern, with chapters and classrooms at the edge producing variations while the center appears intact -> audit where degradation is actually happening (which nodes produce versions, what drives each) rather than establishing canopy-level canon
- The handle is the only control point, not the canopy itself -> trying to control every chapter's output reaches for the canopy, but the handle is the source file -> invest in a canonical asset source that's genuinely easy to use (single location with clearly labeled files for each use case) rather than a governance system
