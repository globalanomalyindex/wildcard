## angles
- Rate metrics compound structural and execution factors (travel distance per pick vs. picks per travel-hour), making interventions site-specific -> measuring separately reveals whether bottleneck is slot assignment vs. picker speed -> decompose the rate metric to target the right factor before adding capacity.
- Monday/Thursday receiving surges create collisions between workflows; the system may be inelastic rather than elastic -> efficiency may not recover fully after receiving ends; lingering pallets, rerouted picking, and supervisor capture persist -> measure recovery time after receiving events to distinguish bounded weekly friction from structural capacity loss.
- The same total receiving volume can be delivered with different temporal shapes -> the peak force on dock-plus-floor capacity is a function of appointment concentration, not total load -> flatten appointment distribution across the week to reduce concurrent peak demand without reducing total work.
- Efficiency loss during receiving cascades through blockages and supervision shifts rather than disappearing -> follow the transfer of effort to specific points: aisle blockage, supervisor reallocation, pick-path lengthening -> identify which transfer vector dominates (e.g., pallets in aisles vs. supervisor capture) to select corresponding interventions (physical separation vs. pre-assigned receiving leads).
