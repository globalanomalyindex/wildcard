## angles
- Aggregate lag number collapses per-partition distribution -> Partition-level lag concentrates on hot buckets while others coast; summing masks the locus -> Pull per-partition lag and offset-commit rates to check if the scaling ceiling is localized rather than uniform.
- Drop rate is a proxy for underlying dynamics -> Rate-of-change matters more than absolute count; lag trajectory toward the 60-second window is the diagnostic -> Monitor rate of change of lag during ramp-up against the latency budget to trigger before the window is compromised.
- Temporal form of accumulation contains a resource-timing lever -> Lag curve has consistent daily form (ramp inflection, peak, overnight drain) that is predictable -> Stage capacity pre-registered before the known inflection point rather than sustained 24-hour posture; targets the bounded 90-minute window.
