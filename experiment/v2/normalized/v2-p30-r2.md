## angles
- 55 lines per hour collapses two independent factors (hands-on picking time vs. transit time) -> measuring only the aggregate hides which is actually the bottleneck -> time a sample of 50 picks split into pick vs. travel; if travel is above 30%, slot scatter is the primary problem; otherwise the issue is elsewhere
- travel time identified as the constraint -> need to rank interventions by yield before taking action -> build a table of top 100 SKUs by frequency with picks-per-week and current distance from pack station, rank by their product, and relocate the top 20 highest-leverage SKUs within 50 feet of pack station
- Monday/Thursday receiving surge delivers the same total volume as a gentler distribution but in sharp spikes -> system can only absorb steady load, not impulses -> restructure appointment slots with carriers so Tuesday and Wednesday each take 20% of weekly volume, reducing Mon/Thu to 30% each
- peak season may bring different order mix than the rest of year -> historical 80 LPH benchmark may not be a valid peak-season target if order composition is simpler -> stratify recent LPH by order complexity and clarify which order types are the real bottleneck before deploying additional capacity
