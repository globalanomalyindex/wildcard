- High-energy zones in glacial deposits are where sorted, coherent material accumulates, not uniformly distributed -> your 70,000 images are non-uniform in licensing value; some shows generate requests constantly, others never -> rank shows by request frequency over three years and apply your three hours per week to the top five shows before anything else, using a ranked triage list by show title
- Glacial stratigraphy becomes ambiguous at boundaries where later events reworked earlier material -> your rights ambiguity clusters around contract regime changes (union negotiations, artistic director transitions, standard contract shifts) rather than distributing evenly across fifteen years -> identify the 2-3 contract history dates that represent regime shifts, flag all folders in ambiguous windows with a single RIGHTS-UNCONFIRMED label so you know within seconds whether a licensing request sits in a clean or flagged zone
- An esker records a persistent channel of one kind of flow sorting one material type cleanly along one path -> marketing's requests cluster into maybe five recurring categories (costume/character shots, solo portraits, set-establishing wides, ensemble scenes, production detail) rather than being random -> run your archive remediation as five sequential passes, one per category, rather than comprehensive show-by-show tagging; each pass delivers complete capability for that request type instead of partial coverage everywhere
