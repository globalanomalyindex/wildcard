## angles
- Fastest movers clustered only by velocity, not by order co-occurrence -> co-occurring SKUs on the same orders create hidden backtracking costs when placed in different aisles -> calculate top 20% of SKU pairs by order co-occurrence frequency and reslot those pairs into adjacent locations within the zone nearest the pack station
- Monday/Thursday receiving collisions masked as busy-day slowdown -> specific throughput hits from spatial contention between receiving and pick flows go untracked in normal operational review -> designate 2-3 aisles as receiving staging zones on those days with explicit time windows (e.g., 7am–noon), pull or defer orders with pick paths in those aisles until after the window closes
- Slotting decisions made by supervisor judgment under receiving pressure -> individual "put it wherever there's space" decisions are locally rational but globally corrosive, and supervisors become the training bottleneck when temps arrive -> assign each warehouse zone a velocity tier (A/B/C) before peak season, add velocity class to receiving process in WMS or on paperwork, route putaway crews by lookup rather than judgment, supervisors approve exceptions only
