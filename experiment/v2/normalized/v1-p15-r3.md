## angles
- Near-zero concentration gradient between central source and local chapters stalls diffusion → the rebrand isn't spreading because chapters have no reason to reach for it when the old files work and are already there → make the new files so dramatically easier to find and use (pre-sized formats, organized by use case, one click away) that the path of least resistance runs through them; a well-organized shared asset folder with ready-to-print files for known chapter use cases shifts the thermodynamics from mandate-and-police to natural adoption.
- Two diffusing substances in a heterogeneous medium coexist on their own gradients rather than fighting to a winner → the rebrand and the old mark are both valid in their respective concentration zones, and the question is whether this coexistence is designed or drifted → map which communication surfaces are in the "donor-chemistry zone" (where the old mark stays for a defined period) and which are in the "new-chapter zone" (new onboarding, new workbooks, digital-first contexts); let the two gradients coexist intentionally as designed superposition rather than compromise.
- In heterogeneous media, low-permeability pockets stay saturated with old substance long after boundary conditions change; you don't clear them, you let them age out on natural schedule → embroidered shirts, decade-old workbooks in classrooms, donated billboard faces are low-permeability pockets that will deplete naturally → concentrate the budget on high-permeability channels (new chapter onboarding kits, digital assets, fresh print runs) where the gradient change is both cheap and durable; sort your contexts by natural refresh rate and put the budget where things turn over fastest.
