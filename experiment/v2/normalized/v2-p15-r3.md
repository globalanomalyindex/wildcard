## angles
- In a resistive medium, diffusion is fastest through high-conductivity nodes rather than uniform broadcast -> identifying the 2-3 chapters with highest print volume and reaching them first with corrected assets -> prioritize outreach (phone calls, thumb drives) to those nodes; budget roughly $500 for this targeted approach
- Two competing versions in the same medium create a confusion zone at the boundary rather than clean displacement -> rather than trying to fully replace the old mark that's already diffused, design a transitional version that retains specific recognition-drivers (hand-lettering weight, color temperature) while adopting new proportions and color system -> commission one canonical file as the chapter-print standard; budget under $2,000
- Material already in wide distribution (printed workbooks in classrooms) cannot be recalled or removed economically -> don't fight the coexistence; formalize it for the active surface going forward -> introduce a low-cost way to acknowledge existing workbooks (rubber-stamp template or stickers reading "Published in our 30th year" on inside cover, roughly $300 for a print run) that frames visual inconsistency as intentional heritage
