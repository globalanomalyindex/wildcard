## angles
- The 460 posts span eight years with a natural age gradient (2018-2020 are "frozen," recent posts are in "active layer") -> publication date is a triage instrument already available -> use a spreadsheet of posts by year cross-referenced against search console traffic loss to rank repair queue by recovery potential without opening individual documents
- Three writers independently covering the same keywords created a cluster problem, not three separate problems; fixing one post while leaving others unfixed leaves the system unstable -> keyword cannibalization requires group treatment -> the minimum viable unit of repair is addressing the entire cluster (consolidate, differentiate, or retire), which may also reduce sign-off bottlenecks by bringing a single decision to product marketing
- The CMS without bulk review only shows surface-visible posts (ones with traffic, links, touched recently); the degraded material is invisible -> you need indirect instruments, not post-by-post inspection -> export search console indexed URLs with traffic and click data, join against a CMS URL list in a spreadsheet, to build a ranked subsidence map of corpus health without developer time or CMS interface work
