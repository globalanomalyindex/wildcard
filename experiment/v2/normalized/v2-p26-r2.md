## angles
- Technique drift in distributed crews only becomes detectable too late (through pod-set rates weeks later) -> The dangerous interval between action and confirmation is where divergence compounds invisibly -> Move the authoritative reference to the point of decision rather than a step away: embed the approved term in the translator's CAT tool so it appears inline as the segment is reached, configured as a shared termbase flag or auto-suggestion, rather than requiring a manual check of a separate glossary
- The long lag between a pollination action and pod confirmation mirrors the lag between marketing team edits to live English pages and downstream detection of mismatches -> The controlled-strip approach: designate the most volatile sections (loyalty-program and key feature-name pages) as a monitored strip and run weekly automated diffs against previous snapshots, triggering targeted notifications to relevant translators before the next job is assigned, scoped only to changed segments
- Detection of a malformed pod is false positive feedback if measured only at harvest; the fix is a binary checkpoint at an intermediate stage -> Downstream customer complaints about mismatched feature names are slow and untraceable signal -> Add three mandatory structured fields to the translator delivery note: (1) the loyalty-program name used in this job, (2) the Japanese app-feature name used, (3) the English source URL and version translated from, converting per-delivery output into a traceable record and enabling constructive conversations about consistency rather than post-complaint blame
