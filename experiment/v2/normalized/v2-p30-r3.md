## angles
- Inventory arriving faster than slotting decisions can be made under dock pressure maps onto the reef ecology pattern of larvae settling when substrate is not ready; the problem is the timing of the decision, not the decision itself -> assign target slots 24-48 hours before truck arrival using ASN data and velocity lookups, so put-away crew executes pre-decided moves instead of making calls on a jammed dock floor
- Slotting by frequency alone misses high-travel offenders; the damaged reef analogy is focusing restoration on high-value substrate in degraded state, not the whole reef -> rank SKUs by pick frequency multiplied by current slot distance from pack stations, work only the top 15-20% where capacity allows, stop there
- Incoming pallets bleeding into pick aisles under dock pressure creates zone fragmentation like habitat disruption across reef boundaries -> designate 2-3 formal buffer staging rows near the dock, sized to absorb a full Monday truck, with dedicated staff moving buffer-to-slot before each pick shift to maintain zone boundary integrity
