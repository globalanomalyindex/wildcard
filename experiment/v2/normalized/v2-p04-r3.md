## angles
- The consumer lag metric (message count) does not reveal whether currently-processed messages are stale or live. It measures eventual accuracy (like a thermometer reaching the right value) but not response lag to real-time events. Precision instruments measure response lag separately. A per-partition gauge of wall-clock minus Kafka record produce-timestamp costs almost nothing and exposes the live/stale boundary precisely, enabling routing decisions that are currently operating blind.
- A second consumer group can seek past the backlog to process only fresh arrivals while the existing group drains backlog overnight, analogous to using both a high-mass primary instrument and a separate low-mass probe to track rapid changes simultaneously. No producer changes or new partitions required; the consumer groups hold independent offsets and the message age gauge indicates when to stop seeking and resume normal consumption.
- Deserialization is the CPU cost bottleneck. If a timestamp is accessible before full JSON parsing (fixed offset, headers, or byte scanning), a thin proxy consumer can classify messages as live or stale before deserializing, routing to separate topics (small live topic, full backlog topic) so the fraud scorer pulls only fresh data during peak hours and wastes no deserialization budget on stale messages.
