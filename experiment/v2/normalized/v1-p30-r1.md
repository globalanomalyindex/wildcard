## angles
- Fastest-moving SKUs assigned to random slots inversely correlated with travel cost, creating frequency-rank inversion -> this is an architectural problem solvable by ranking actual picks-per-day against location travel-time and reassigning to mirror those ranks -> pull 90 days of pick data per SKU, rank both by frequency and by travel cost, then slot the top 5% of SKUs to golden-zone locations where the frequency cliff is steep enough to recover most lost throughput
- Incomplete re-slotting during peak volume creates a hybrid-state warehouse where pickers hold two conflicting maps simultaneously under cognitive load, which is worse than a single bad map -> the problem is timing and commit discipline, not speed -> set a hard freeze date by week five or six after which no slotting changes happen until after peak, with all design decisions and physical moves committed before the freeze
- Inbound pallets on receiving days compete with picker travel in the same aisles without pre-assigned separation, producing the warehouse equivalent of two overlapping audio tracks -> the fix is structural routing separation committed before the surge, not reactive workarounds -> designate specific aisles as receiving corridors on Monday and Thursday, route pick operations around them in advance, and use the predictable two-day cluster to commit the channel separation schedule once and run it weekly
