## angles
- Folder names, filenames, and modification dates are artifacts of how files were ingested, not what images they contain. They obscure rather than reveal the true structure. -> Use the EXIF DateTimeOriginal field (written by the camera) as ground truth instead. -> For the wedding photo hunt, run exiftool with a date filter across both drives to recover the full set in one pass, entirely offline.
- Same-stack cutting in marquetry produces shape-identical pieces across color variants. -> In a photo collection, perceptual hashing clusters images by visual content regardless of resolution, compression, or color grading, identifying duplicates at different "dress". -> Use perceptual-hash clustering (e.g., czkawka or digiKam) to identify confirmed duplicate clusters, then delete redundants with certainty that the visual content exists elsewhere.
- The donkey technique prevents misalignment by processing the whole stack before sorting individual pieces. -> The paralysis comes from treating this as a photo-by-photo decision problem; two collection-level passes transform it into a cluster-level decision problem. -> Run EXIF-date filtering first (wedding cohort extraction), then perceptual-hash clustering across all sources; only after these passes do targeted moves on confirmed duplicates.
