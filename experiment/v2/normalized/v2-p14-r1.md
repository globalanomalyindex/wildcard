## angles
- 140 failures are not one population; split by (a) remediation type (code/ARIA/semantic vs. wording change) and (b) scope (global/shared-component vs. single-screen) -> code-only fixes should not enter legal review pipeline, and shared-component fixes propagate across 60+ screens in one deployment -> classify failures by these two axes before sprint planning to sequence legal batches efficiently over the 9-month window
- The unowned shared header is a dead zone accumulating WCAG failures that propagate to downstream screens and re-fail in testing -> without explicit ownership, re-failures reset court trust and worsen debt rather than merely delay remediation -> designate one party as accountable owner of shared header remediation with a written, specific scope contract for the full 9-month period
- Automated scanners probe certain failure types (color contrast, missing alt text) but miss session-timeout behavior, focus traps in multi-step forms, and announcement order under dynamic state changes -> highest-impact barriers for users on library computers with 15-minute sessions may be invisible to the audit -> cross-reference the 140 failures against housing and food assistance application submission paths end-to-end, flag screens not in the list, and manually audit those for focus management and session-timeout behavior
