Drawing on the concept of adaptation — specifically how adaptations become liabilities when the environment they were built for changes — a few moves surfaced that seem worth offering.

---

**1. Decode the 2019 folder scheme before you touch it.**

Your pre-2019 folder structure was well-adapted to how you were managing photos then — some organizing principle made sense at the time (by event, by date, by camera, by person). Since 2019 your collection grew on platforms with entirely different logic, and now the old scheme reads as noise. But the scheme has a key, even if you've forgotten it. Spend 20 minutes reconstructing what it was tracking: open the root of one of the drives, look at the folder names, and write down the rule in one sentence ("folders are named YYYY-MM-DD\_EventName" or "one folder per year, subfolders per trip"). Once you have that key, you can issue a single targeted search for "wedding" or your brother's name or the month of the wedding rather than browsing manually. This gives you a precise entry point without reorganizing anything or moving a single file.

---

**2. Target the edited/original pairs first — they're the easiest decision in the collection.**

The harder deduplication problem is "same photo, different resolution across four pools" — that requires a tool and judgment. But the edited/original pairs are structurally different and much easier: they're typically same filename, same date, different file size, sitting in the same folder or in adjacent ones. On each external drive, run a quick filter for that signature (a tool like `fdupes`, `jdupes`, or even the macOS Finder "sort by name" view in a date range can surface these). Clearing the obvious edit/original pairs first reduces the noise before you attempt the harder cross-platform dedup pass — and since you can see exactly which one is the lightly-resized export and which is the full original, the risk of accidentally deleting the only good copy is near zero for this subset specifically.

---

**3. Search Google Photos for your brother's face before reorganizing anything.**

Google Photos already has facial recognition running on everything you've uploaded, and that model was not built for your 2019 folder scheme — it built its own index. Open People (or use the search bar and type your brother's name if he's been tagged) and pull everything Google Photos has already associated with him. This takes under two minutes, doesn't touch your drives or your iCloud account, and doesn't consume any upload bandwidth. The gap between what that search returns and what you remember from the wedding — the shots that aren't there — tells you exactly which photos are still only on one of the external drives, so you know the scope of what still needs to be found rather than searching blindly across all four pools at once.
